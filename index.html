About Me

My name is Owen Rasor, and I am a Software Engineering student at Utah Valley University. In addition to my studies, I have built a small business called XTRAJANK, where I design and screen print unique apparel. I personally manage everything from design and printing to selling at local markets. This experience has strengthened my ability to think creatively, solve problems efficiently, and adapt to new challenges.

I have worked on a variety of software development projects, including:

  • Memory Simulator (Python, kivy): A virtual machine that executes Basic Machine Language and simulates memory management.
  • File Transfer Appliction (Python, tkinter): A program for secure and efficient file transfers.
  • Simple Banking Application (C#): A system for account management.
  • Circular Buffer (C++): A high performance data structure for managing a fixed-size buffer.

In addition to Python, C++, and C#, I have experience with C, .NET, Kivy, Tkinter, and Git. I enjoy working on both low-level systems programming and GUI development, allowing me to create intuitive and functional applications.

On the creative side, I have screen printed hundreds of designs on t-shirts, hoodies, blankets, and tote bags. I find fulfillment in both coding and design, as both require problem-solving, attention to detail, and innovation.

My Goals

My goal is to become a software engineer where I can apply my creativity, knowledge, and skills to build meaningful applications. I am passionate about designing efficient, user-friendly software and constantly seeking opportunities to grow and learn in the field.
